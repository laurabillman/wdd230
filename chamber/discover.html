DISCOVER SYRACUSE!

Syracuse is the 35th most populated city in the state of Utah, with a population of 31,715 people. Syracuse racial/ethnic groups are White (87.0%), Hispanic (6.1%), and Two or More (2.9%). The public schools in Syracuse are above average. In 2022, the median household income in Syracuse was $114,547. Living in Syracuse offers residents a rural feel and most residents own their homes. In Syracuse there are a lot of parks. Many families live in Syracuse and residents tend to be conservative.

Syracuse City MuseumCome discover the history and founding of Syracuse City at the Syracuse Museum! The Syracuse Regional Museum celebrates and preserves the local history of the city of Syracuse and its early citizens. The museum, located near City Hall, tells the story of the early social, economic, and cultural development of Syracuse and the surrounding areas. The museum complex consists of the Main Building, the Barn, the Waite Barbershop, and the historic Wilcox Cabin.

Jensen Nature ParkVisit Jensen Nature Park and duck pond. This tranquil Nature Park features many amenities and facilities including a 0.59-mile walking and jogging trail, Fishing, Orienteering course, Pavilions with barbeque and picnic tables, and trail access. Come enjoy nature, the beautiful scenery, and wildlife!

Black Island FarmsBlack Island Farms is a family owned, 60 years of business working vegetable farm, with about 300 acres in cultivation! Harvesting carrots, onions, cabbage, corn, pumpkins, squash and gourds. Bring your family to the farm this fall! Come hungry and enjoy farm-fresh goodies from our market! Attractions: 12 acre Corn Maze, Animal Alley, Bonfires, Cow Train, Grain Shoot Slide, Straw Mountain, Pig Races, Corn Box, Bounce House, Pumpkin Patch, Farm Hayrides, Nightmare Acres, Haunted barn and so much more!

As the largest saline lake in the Western Hemisphere, the Great Salt Lake is crucial to both people and nature. Antelope Island State ParkSyracuse is the gateway to Utah's Antelope Isand Stae Park! Antelope Island is rich in scenic beauty and natural features, Antelope Island is the perfect place to view the Great Salt Lake and get a taste of the vast open country found in the Great Basin. Antelope Isalnd is home to many type of animals, including: buffalo, deer, bobcats, coyotes, many varieties of birds and waterfowl. Recreational opportunities abound. White sand beaches beckon to sunbathers and picnickers. Hiking, biking and horseback trails lead to scenic points on the island. A marina serves sailboats, powerboats, and kayakers. Campers come to watch beautiful sunrises and sunsets in seclusion found only minutes from their urban homes.
